About

Julie is a front-end developer that is familiar in in front-end and back-end development. She is a current student in the Udacity front-end nanodegree interested in finding an entry level position as a web or UI developer. She has worked in Javascript, CSS3, HTML5, ES6, jQuery, MERN stack, Sass and Python.

Her projects at the moment are as follows. The first one is a random sentence generator based on a corpus gathered from the short stories of Sherlock Holmes. This project implements OOP in Python and a few basic data structures. Another project is an animal trading card built on HTML5 and CSS3 where an animal's main characteristics are explained and displayed in an organized manner.

Julie's greatest interests from the moment she inquired into web development are focused on semantic application of design and proper layout of websites. In her free time she designs code snippets to test her CSS/HTML abilities and expand them.

Previous to becoming a student of Udacity, she graduated as a BS Electronics Engineer. She discovered her true passion for programming by building an autonomous robot and programming it as well as programming a robotic arm for her thesis.
